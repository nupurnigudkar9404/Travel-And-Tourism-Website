Days Plan

  • Day 1

    Arival at Jaipur


    Arrival in Jaipur: Land in Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, known as the "Pink City." Check-in: Check into your hotel and freshen up. City Palace: Explore the historic City Palace, a magnificent complex of palaces, courtyards, and gardens showcasing Rajasthani and Mughal architecture. Jantar Mantar: Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Jantar Mantar, an astronomical observatory featuring impressive astronomical instruments. Hawa Mahal: Admire the iconic Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), known for its intricate latticework fa√ßade. Capture photos of this architectural marvel as the sun sets.
  • Day 2

    Amber Fort & Sightseeing


    Amber Fort: Head to the outskirts of Jaipur to explore the majestic Amber Fort. Take an elephant ride or jeep ride up to the fort and marvel at its impressive ramparts, palaces, and courtyards. Jaigarh Fort: Visit Jaigarh Fort, located near Amber Fort. Explore the fortifications, museum, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Shopping: Spend the evening shopping for traditional Rajasthani handicrafts, textiles, and jewelry in Jaipur's vibrant bazaars such as Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar.
  • Day 3

    Jodhpur


    Drive to Jodhpur: Depart for Jodhpur, known as the "Blue City," renowned for its historic forts and vibrant culture. Mehrangarh Fort: Upon arrival, visit Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India, dominating the city skyline. Explore its palaces, courtyards, and museums. Jaswant Thada: Visit Jaswant Thada, a stunning marble cenotaph built in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II. Admire its intricate carvings and serene surroundings. Old City Exploration: Take a leisurely walk through the narrow lanes of the old city, soaking in the ambiance and vibrant colors of Jodhpur's blue-painted houses.
  • Day 4

    Jaisalmer


    Drive to Jaisalmer: Depart for Jaisalmer, the "Golden City," known for its magnificent sandstone fort and desert landscapes. En route Stop: Visit the ancient village of Osian, known for its beautifully sculpted Jain temples dating back to the 8th century. Jaisalmer Fort: Upon arrival, explore Jaisalmer Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Marvel at its intricate architecture, narrow alleys, and stunning views of the Thar Desert. Sunset at Sam Sand Dunes: Head to the Sam Sand Dunes to witness a mesmerizing sunset over the desert. Enjoy camel rides and cultural performances amidst the sand dunes.
  • Day 5

    Rabindra Sarobar:


    Start your day with a visit to the Academy of Fine Arts, a cultural institution showcasing works of art by renowned artists. Explore its galleries and attend any ongoing exhibitions. Rabindra Sarobar: Enjoy a morning walk or leisurely boat ride at Rabindra Sarobar, a tranquil lake surrounded by lush greenery. Treat your taste buds to the flavors of Bengali cuisine at a traditional restaurant. Marble Palace: Explore the opulent Marble Palace, known for its exquisite architecture, impressive art collection, and lush gardens.
  • Day 5

    Gadisar Lake


    Patwon ki Haveli: Visit Patwon ki Haveli, a cluster of elaborately carved mansions showcasing Rajasthani craftsmanship and architecture. Gadisar Lake: Explore Gadisar Lake, an artificial reservoir surrounded by temples and ghats. Take a boat ride or leisurely stroll along the lake's shores. Cultural Evening: Experience a cultural evening in Jaisalmer, featuring traditional music and dance performances under the starlit sky.
  • Day 6

    Udaipur


    Drive to Udaipur: Depart for Udaipur, the "City of Lakes," famous for its romantic ambiance and stunning palaces. City Palace: Visit the majestic City Palace complex, overlooking Lake Pichola. Explore its palaces, museums, and enjoy panoramic views of the city and lake. Boat Ride on Lake Pichola: Enjoy a boat ride on Lake Pichola, passing by iconic landmarks such as Jag Mandir and the Lake Palace
  • Day 7

    Departure from Rajasthan


    Conclude your Rajasthan journey with a farewell dinner at a rooftop restaurant, overlooking the illuminated city of Udaipur.
